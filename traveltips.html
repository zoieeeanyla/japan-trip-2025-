📌 Quick Travel Tips

Helpful advice to make your trip smooth & fun!

🚆 Transportation

  • Use Suica or Pasmo cards for all local Tokyo trains and subways — reload at stations or convenience stores.
  • Arrive 30 mins early on Shinkansen day to navigate and grab snacks.
  • For airport and Mt. Fuji trips, use private car services to avoid stress with luggage.

🍱 Food & Meals

  • Mix it up: convenience store breakfast, street food lunch, 1 sit-down dinner.
  • Top family-friendly spots: Ichiran, Sushi-Ro, Tonkatsu Maisen, Gusto, Komeda Coffee.
  • Use Google Maps reviews & look for lines — in Japan, lines = good food!

🎟️ Attractions

  • Book ahead for Mario Kart, teamLab, DisneySea — keep digital copies of tickets.
  • Joypolis & Ueno Zoo are pay-on-arrival — avoid peak weekend times.
  • Carry coins for arcades, gachapon, and shrine boxes!

💸 Money Tips

  • Bring cash — many places are cash-only.
  • Use 7-Eleven or Japan Post ATMs for international cards.
  • Set a daily cap of ¥9,000–10,000 per person to stay under $300/day.

🧳 Packing Essentials

  • Pack good walking shoes, folding umbrellas, water bottles, and snack bags.
  • Use a backpack for day trips (hands-free is best!).
  • Packing cubes = your new best friend.

📱 Connectivity

  • Use an eSIM or pocket Wi-Fi for maps, bookings, and Google Translate.
  • Download: Google Maps, Navitime Japan Travel, Google Translate (offline).

⭐ Extra Recommendations

  • Always carry tissues and hand sanitizer — not all restrooms have them.
  • Respect the quiet: avoid talking loudly on public transportation.
  • Try at least one capsule toy (gachapon) and one themed café!
